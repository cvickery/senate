Statement:

I kindly request to serve on the Undergraduate Scholastic Standards Committee, which is organized to uphold and review the policies and procedures governing academic standards and consider student appeals accordingly. As Queens College alum, I value first-hand the mission for which the College was created: to offer a strong liberal arts education to working class people. I am an immigrant; I am the first with a college degree in my family; and my relationship with Queens College spans 18 years. I understand the hardships facing our unique student body. In addition, my scholarly work and experience outside of Queens College have allowed me to see the need for motivating our students to higher excellence. I have an interest in how the College can leverage scholastic standards to significantly improve student success. This semester I will hold a graduate student symposium to afford students the opportunity to extend their acade! mic performance to a public sphere. I would equally like to see undergraduate students also held to higher expectations. I understand and respect students’ time constraints and family obligations, but I mentor my undergraduate students to raise the bar on what they consider a strong resume so that they can compete for the best graduate programs or excel in the job market. The success of our students is not only personally rewarding, but ultimately benefits the college. My service to the Committee would involve balancing empathy for the student and a commitment to the standards that will ensure that a QC degree continues to be held in high regard.
