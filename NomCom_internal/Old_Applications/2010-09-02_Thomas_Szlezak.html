Statement:

As a Student Aide at the Academic Advising Center and an alum of the Peer Counseling Program, I have run across students of academic situations that run the gamut. My experience in dealing with these different populations and thorough understanding of the current curriculum would help me to assess the current and future curriculum for undergraduate students. As a current alternate senator and prospective administrator in higher education, I believe that a strong curriculum can be of value to every student. Through the Advising Center, I've also assisted with the welcoming of new students from the Admissions office, who despite coming from different backgrounds, must all meet the same general requirements upon graduation. I am also willing to serve on the Special Committee for Technology and Library. I feel Queens College has much to do to keep up on the cusp of technology, and to make all its technological resources more fully available to the large (and growing) population. The library on the other hand, is a powerful resource that not many students really seem to take advantage of. The priorities of these two resources need to be discussed and revised.
